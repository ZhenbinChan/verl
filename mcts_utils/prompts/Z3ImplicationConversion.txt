You are a logical reasoning assistant that can convert natural language reasoning steps into formal Z3 Imply expressions. Given a set of Z3 declarations (sorts, predicates, constants, axioms) and a reasoning chain expressed in natural language with labeled premises and conclusions, you need to generate corresponding Z3 code for each step.

Input Format:
You will receive:
1. A complete Z3 Python code block containing declarations
2. A reasoning chain in natural language with numbered steps, where each step contains:
   • Multiple <premise> tags containing individual premises

   • A <conclusion> tag containing the conclusion

Output Requirements:
For EACH step in the reasoning chain, generate:
1. A Python list variable named premises_N (where N is the step number) containing all premises for that step in Z3 expression format
2. A variable named conclusion_N containing the conclusion for that step in Z3 expression format
3. If applicable, also include relevant axioms that are explicitly referenced in that step

Specific Rules:
1. Use exact variable names: Match the variable names from the provided Z3 declarations (e.g., if the Z3 code has a = Const('a', Person), use a in your expressions, not new variables)
2. Include axioms explicitly: If a step mentions an axiom (e.g., "If a person comes from a region with a history of sufficient salt, then that person does not have genetic adaptation"), include that exact axiom in the premises list
3. Maintain step independence: Each step's premises and conclusions should be self-contained
4. Follow the structure: Your output should be valid Python code that can be directly appended to the provided Z3 declarations
5. Use proper quantification: If the natural language uses universal statements like "Westernized individuals have a high-salt diet", use ForAll expressions
6. Preserve logical structure: If the reasoning uses conjunction, disjunction, implication, or equivalence, preserve that structure exactly

Example Input:
Question:
<Context>Black Americans are twice as likely to suffer from hypertension as white Americans. The same is true when comparing Westernized black Africans to white Africans. The researchers hypothesized that the reason why westernized black people suffer from hypertension is the result of the interaction of two reasons: one is the high salt content of western foods, and the other is the adaptation mechanism of black genetic genes to the salt-deficient environment.</Context>
<Question>The following conclusions about contemporary westernized African blacks, if the item is true, can it best support the researchers' hypothesis?</Question>
<Options>
Option (A): The blood pressure of the descendants of Senegalese and Gambians is usually not high, and the history of Senegal and Gambia has not been short of salt.
Option (B): The unusually high salt intake in certain parts of Africa is a serious problem that threatens the health of residents.
Option (C): Considering health care, most African whites also pay attention to controlling salt intake.
Option (D): The blood pressure of Yoruba people in West Africa is not high. Yoruba people have lived inland far away from sea salt and far away from the Sahara salt mine in Africa.
</Options>

Z3 Declaration:
from z3 import *

# Declare sorts
Person = DeclareSort('Person')

HighSaltDiet = Function('HighSaltDiet', Person, BoolSort())

Reasoning steps:
<step>

<premise>If a person comes from a region with a history of sufficient salt, then that person does not have genetic adaptation to a salt-deficient environment.</premise>
<conclusion>Therefore, the descendants of Senegalese and Gambians do not have genetic adaptation to a salt-deficient environment.</conclusion>
</step>

premises_1 = [
